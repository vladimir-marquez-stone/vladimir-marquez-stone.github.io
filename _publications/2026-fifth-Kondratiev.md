This work aims to verify the existence of an expansive wave corresponding to the fifth long cycle emerged in the United States after the 1973-1975 crisis. This study calculates the rate of profit, the rate of surplus value, and the organic composition of capital (OCC). The long-run movements are extracted with an Hodrick–Prescott filter and analyzed using a Vector Error Correction Model and Bai–Perron breakpoint tests. The results indicate the onset of a new expansive wave after 1983, driven in the first instance by a sharp increase in the rate of surplus value amid labor compensation reduction, and in the second instance by a slowdown in OCC growth associated with productivity gains from the fourth technological revolution.
